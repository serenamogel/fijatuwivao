Julianna Margulies Career

Julianna made her feature film debut in 1991, in Steven Seagal’s film Out for Justice , playing a prostitute.

Julianna Margulies ER – Julianna Margulies George Clooney

In 1994, she was cast in the pilot episode of the NBC medical drama ER as Carol Hathaway, an emergency care nurse who, despondent over her relationship with pediatrician Doug Ross a role played by George Clooney, attempted suicide. Her character as Carol Hathaway was originally intended to die, but the producers changed the plot and made her a main cast member. She was at the same time offered an extended role on NBC’s Homicide: Life on the Streets, after a two-episode appearance earlier in the year. She however chose ER and remained on the show for six seasons until 2000. In 1994, she won an Emmy Award for Best Supporting Actress in a Television Drama , and continued to be nominated for this award every year during her tenure on ER. Julianna was the only series regular cast member to win an Emmy Award.

Julianna Margulies The Good Wife

Margulies started starring in the CBS legal drama The Good Wife, in 2009. She was cast on the role of Alicia Florrick, an attorney returning to legal practice after her husband Peter Florrick, the role played by Chris Noth resigned as Illinois State’s Attorney amid a sex and corruption scandal. Margulies was credited as a producer of The Good Wife, as of 2011, starting with the third season. The series came to an end in May 2016.

Julianna Margulies Scrubs

She made a guest appearance in 2004, in a two-episode arc in season 4 of the hit TV show Scrubs as Neena Broderick, an unscrupulous lawyer who sues Turk and has a brief sexual relationship with J.D. She also starred in another miniseries on TNT, The Grid.

Julianna Margulies Sopranos

She appeared in four episodes of the sixth season of The Sopranos, in April 2006, portraying realtor Julianna Skiff. In 2006 in the month of August, she was cast in Snakes on a Plane as flight attendant Claire Miller. In December 2006, she was cast on the role of Jennifer Bloom in the Syfy Channel miniseries The Lost Room.
