Talented singer Lyfe Jennings lives a comfortable life with a net worth of $500 thousand.

Jennings returned to his artistic ambitions in 2002 after completing ten years in prison.

After a fight with ex-fiancé Joy Bounds in October 2008, he tracked her down to her family’s home in Smyrna, Georgia.

He smashed the door to the house, fired rounds into the road, and afterward led police on a 90-mph chase that stopped when his 2005 Corvette crashed.

Lyfe Jennings | Career

Lyfe Jennings created a four-song demo CD two days following his release from prison in December 2002.

He appeared at the Apollo a month after his release. He was booed as he stepped onto the stage, but his harsh vocal and poetic lyricism won over the Apollo audience.

Lyfe followed up with a hip-hop-focused album called “The Phoenix,” which debuted in August 2006, with special guests feature from Three 6 Mafia and Young Buck.

His next album, “I Still Believe,” was released in August 2010.

Jennings had stated that “I Still Believe” would be his final album; however, he did not follow through on this promise.

“Lucid” marked his return to music in October 2013. Like his prior studio albums, it debuted in the Top 10 of Billboard’s R&B chart.

In June 2015, he released “Tree of Lyfe,” mostly recorded in his home studio.

Moreover, in 2019, he released a new album “777.”
