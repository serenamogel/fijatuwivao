Garcia Eddie Meteorologist Young

His earliest memories of the weather start right here in the Land of Enchantment. He remembers being fascinated by the broad range of weather in New Mexico. The pure winter snow atop Ski Apache in Ruidos to the refreshing summer Monsoon rain traveling through Albuquerque and Santa Fe.

Little did he know when he grew up, his career would bring him here to KOB-TV in September 2010 – the best move of his life. Here, Eddie feels connected to the people of New Mexico, the culture, and the breath-taking natural beauty.

Garcia began his career at the age of 17, working behind the cameras at KMLM-TV in Midland, Texas, and KEYE-TV in Austin, Texas. By the age of 23, he decided to cross over to reporting while he was a news photographer at KOSA-TV in Odessa, Texas.

Eddie has won numerous awards for his coverage of Hurricanes Katrina and Ike, tornadoes, wildfires, floods, and more. New Mexico has always been a part of Eddie’s life,  his family continues to thrive in the Land of Enchantment.

He came to KOB in September 2010 as a general assignments reporter but quickly transitioned to weather forecasting in 2012. He received his Broadcast Meteorology Certification from Mississippi State University.
